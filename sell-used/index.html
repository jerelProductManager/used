Navigation & discovery
Source: Medallia nav click data + feedback
"Sell Used" added to primary nav. Currently absent — one of the most-clicked items historically but discovery-dependent. Persistent nav link removes that dependency.
Hero CTA with search bar. Puts the seller into the flow immediately without requiring them to navigate to a separate page.
4-step progress bar
Source: GA4 funnel — 66% drop-off at basket
Process transparency upfront. Sellers who understand the flow before starting are less likely to abandon mid-flow. Sets expectations on commitment level (non-binding at basket).
Trade-in credit value prop on landing
Source: Medallia — credit confusion + competitive analysis
Currently buried until confirmation email. The tax-free credit advantage is B&H's strongest competitive differentiator — it should lead, not trail. MPB and KEH have no equivalent.
"See how it compares" link pre-empts competitive shopping by offering B&H's own comparison. Signals transparency — consistent with B&H's top brand attribute: "trust and honesty."

Type-ahead with price previews
Source: GA4 — 2nd largest funnel drop-off at search step
Current state: keyword input, no feedback until full search. Users have no signal whether their item is in the system or what it's worth until after they search — causing early abandonment.
Price preview in type-ahead. "Up to $1,820" shown inline gives users instant quote range signal, reducing exit to competitor sites for a preliminary quote comparison.
AND-based semantic search
Source: Medallia + internal engineering analysis
Current: OR-based Solr search. "Sony A7 IV mirrorless" returns anything matching any single word — noisy, irrelevant results are the #1 friction point.
Proposed: AND/semantic hybrid. Phase 1 moves to BM25 AND-logic; Phase 2 adds vector embeddings for intent-based matching ("portrait camera" finds A7 IV). Covered in architecture review.
Custom quote escape hatch
Source: Medallia — obscure/niche item frustration
Surfaced at Step 1, not buried. Currently users who can't find their item have no clear path forward. Early visibility of the custom quote option prevents abandonment on niche gear.

New condition scale
Source: Medallia + internal condition rating initiative
Current: dual numeric scales. Sell side uses 1–5; buy side uses 7–10+. Creates a trust and comprehension gap when sellers compare their quote to the buy listing.
New word-based scale: Excellent → Rough. Same language across buy and sell sides. Long-form descriptions (shown in gray box) help sellers self-assess accurately, reducing quote adjustment surprises at inspection.
Competitive comparison widget
Source: Meeting ideation — "Kayak for used gear quotes"
A significant portion of funnel drop-off is price-shopping. Sellers leave to check MPB and KEH mid-flow. This widget keeps them on-site by surfacing competitor estimates transparently.
B&H's competitive lever is the credit multiplier. Even if cash offer is comparable, the $1,804 credit vs $1,640 cash gap — amplified by tax-free trade-in — makes B&H the clear winner when the math is shown. ⚠ Feasibility of real-time competitor API TBD — static estimates acceptable for MVP.

Why store credit is worth more at B&H
Store credit for trade-ins at B&H is tax-free in New York and California — meaning your $1,640 quote in credit effectively buys the same as $1,804+ in after-tax cash. Add the tax savings on future purchases, and the gap widens further. No other used gear buyer — MPB, KEH, or UsedPhotoPro — offers an equivalent benefit.
You can change your payment preference any time before we finalize your quote.
Payment method moved to Step 3
Source: Medallia — payment friction cluster
Current state: payment revealed in confirmation email. Customers complete the entire flow before learning their options. This is a major contributor to post-submission dissatisfaction and repeat contact.
Moving to Step 3 of 4 serves two goals: (1) It's a natural re-engagement hook — users who were drifting re-commit when they see concrete payment options. (2) It eliminates a major source of post-quote friction.
Credit value quantified in dollars
Source: Medallia — trade-in credit confusion cluster
"Worth more" isn't enough. Medallia data shows customers don't understand the actual upside. The badge reads "$1,804 effective value" — not "10% bonus" — because concrete dollar amounts convert better than percentages.
Tax-free framing is the differentiator. The explainer card contextualizes the NY/CA tax exemption in seller-friendly language. This is B&H's unique moat — it should be named explicitly, not implied.

"Non-binding" disambiguation
Source: GA4 — 66%+ drop-off at add-to-basket
Root cause of basket drop-off. Qualitative Medallia data and meeting analysis suggest many users abandon because they believe adding to basket is a binding commitment to sell. It isn't — but nothing in the current UI says so.
Two interventions: (1) Step bar on every screen sets context early. (2) Explicit "non-binding" copy directly below the CTA at basket — appears exactly where anxiety peaks.
Post-drop-off "shipment received" email
Source: Internal audit — missing communication touchpoint
Critical missing touchpoint. Currently there is no communication between the seller dropping off their package and B&H issuing a quote. This is a multi-day silence that generates anxiety and drives inbound support contacts.
Email #2 ("Shipment received") closes the gap. Showing the full 4-email sequence upfront at basket also sets expectations, reducing inbound "where is my quote" contacts before they occur.
Multi-item basket
Source: Competitive analysis — KEH, MPB support multi-item
KEH and MPB both support multi-item sell baskets. B&H sellers often want to offload a kit (body + lenses). A basket model increases average order value and reduces friction for multi-item sellers who'd otherwise submit multiple separate requests.

Mobile opportunity
Source: GA4 — 185K desktop, 29K mobile (12 months)
Mobile is an untapped 14% of current traffic — but drop-off rates mirror desktop, suggesting the problem is awareness and experience quality, not intent. Sellers using phones to photograph gear for a custom quote are a natural mobile use case not currently served.
Photo upload for custom quotes is the highest-leverage mobile-specific feature. A seller who can photograph scratches or damage directly in the sell flow removes the back-and-forth with evaluators and improves quote accuracy. Feasibility TBD with architecture team.
Mobile-specific UX adaptations
Prototype notes
Condition selection → vertical list on mobile. The 5-column grid used on desktop doesn't translate to small screens. A vertical list with condition name, color indicator, and price on the same row is more tap-friendly.
Competitor comparison simplified. On mobile, the full 3-competitor widget is condensed to B&H vs MPB (closest competitor by volume) with a "view full comparison" option. Avoids horizontal scroll.
Payment method → stacked cards. The 3-column payment grid becomes full-width stacked cards on mobile, with the recommended option (Store Credit) leading and its credit amount prominently displayed.
